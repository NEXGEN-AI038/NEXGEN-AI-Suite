# 🧪 Liver Function Test (LFT)

## What is an LFT?

A Liver Function Test (LFT) is a group of blood tests used to assess how well the liver is functioning. These tests measure enzymes, proteins, and substances produced or processed by the liver.

---

## Common Parameters

- ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase)
- AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase)
- ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase)
- GGT (Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase)
- Total Bilirubin
- Direct Bilirubin
- Albumin
- Total Protein

---

## Why Doctors Order an LFT

Healthcare providers may request an LFT to:

- Evaluate liver health
- Monitor liver disease
- Check medication effects on the liver
- Investigate jaundice
- Assess alcohol-related liver damage

---

## Educational Note

An abnormal LFT does not automatically mean there is serious liver disease.

Results should always be interpreted alongside symptoms, medical history, physical examination, and other investigations.
